As of August 2025, several significant laws have been enacted in India across various sectors. Here's an overview of the key legislation:

1. Promotion and Regulation of Online Gaming Bill, 2025

This landmark legislation bans all online games involving monetary transactions, including fantasy sports platforms like Dream11 and Mobile Premier League (MPL). The law aims to protect consumers from financial harm and prevent misuse for money laundering and terror financing. Consequently, major platforms have begun shutting down their real-money gaming operations in India.

2. The Constitution (130th Amendment) Bill, 2025

This bill introduces provisions to remove ministers from office if they are arrested and detained in custody for serious criminal offenses. It applies to the Prime Minister, Chief Ministers, and other ministers at both the central and state levels. The legislation has been passed by Parliament and is awaiting presidential assent.

3. Income-Tax (No.2) Bill, 2025 & Taxation Laws (Amendment) Bill, 2025

These bills have been passed by Parliament and are awaiting presidential assent. The Income-Tax (No.2) Bill, 2025, aims to amend the Income-Tax Act, 1961, while the Taxation Laws (Amendment) Bill, 2025, seeks to amend various taxation laws to enhance compliance and streamline procedures.

4. Indian Institutes of Management (Amendment) Bill, 2025

This bill amends the Indian Institutes of Management Act, 2017, to enhance the autonomy and governance of Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs). It has been passed by both Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha.

5. Merchant Shipping Act, 2025

Replacing the 1958 Act, this legislation modernizes India's maritime legal framework, aligning it with international conventions. It aims to enhance safety, environmental protection, and ease of doing business in the shipping sector. The bill has been passed by Parliament and is awaiting presidential assent.

6. National Sports Governance Act, 2025

This Act establishes a statutory framework for the governance of national sports bodies in India. It aims to promote transparency, accountability, and ethical practices in sports administration. The bill has been passed by Parliament and is awaiting presidential assent. 
Wikipedia

7. Indian Ports Act, 2025

Replacing the colonial-era Indian Ports Act, 1908, this legislation provides a modern legal framework for port governance in India. It aims to promote integrated development, environmental protection, and cooperative federalism between the Centre and States. The bill has been passed by Parliament and is awaiting presidential assent.

8. Carriage of Goods by Sea Act, 2025

This Act replaces the 1925 legislation and consolidates the law relating to the carriage of goods by sea. It aims to give effect to international conventions on bills of lading and promote uniformity in maritime transport rules. The bill has been passed by Parliament and is awaiting presidential assent.
